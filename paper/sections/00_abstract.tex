\begin{abstract}
Project 21 isolated a reduced shared\_B selector inside the Gap A answer-pair generator problem.  In the 16-candidate reduced universe, native shell labels and native shell ranks select exactly the four observed shared\_B cycles, with no false positives and no misses.  The visible selector appears as the permutation \([1,2,0,3]=(0\ 1\ 2)(3)\), but in lifted shell/rank coordinates it becomes the identity rule: same shell and same rank.

This paper introduces the Lift \& Twist conjecture.  The weak form is a coordinate-straightening theorem inside the reduced universe.  The strong form conjectures that the reduced 16-candidate universe itself is the shadow of one local answer cell under shell lift and phase twist.  The paper does not claim Gap A closure.  Its purpose is to make the next native-generator target precise.
\end{abstract}
