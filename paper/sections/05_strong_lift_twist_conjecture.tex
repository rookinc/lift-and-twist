\section{Strong Lift \& Twist conjecture}

The reduced theorem explains the selector after the reduced universe is given.  The next question is whether Lift \& Twist generates that universe.

\begin{conjecture}[Strong Lift \& Twist conjecture]
The reduced 16-candidate shared\_B universe is not an arbitrary product of observed C-cycle support and observed anchor-cycle support.  It is the two-readout shadow of one local answer cell under shell lift and phase twist.
\end{conjecture}

In this conjecture, the 16 candidates arise as
\[
    \{\text{four lifted C-readout states}\}
    \times
    \{\text{four twisted anchor-readout states}\}.
\]
The four selected cycles are the hidden diagonal.

The strongest possible extension would be a Gap A Lift \& Twist closure theorem: Lift \& Twist generates the full role-labeled shared\_B edge universe natively, and the reciprocal answer-pair grammar then recovers exactly the 12 WXYZTI answer-pairs with no false positives and no misses.  This paper does not assert that theorem.
